Welcome! This website is a place to share my research, projects, and occasional updates throughout my PhD.

My research focuses on multimessenger astrophysics, combining gravitational-wave and X-ray observations to better understand the formation and evolution of compact objects. I am currently investigating the apparent discrepancy between the high black hole spins measured in High-Mass X-ray Binaries (HMXBs) and the generally lower spins inferred from gravitational-wave observations of the binary black holes they may evolve into.

Alongside this, I work on likelihood-free inference techniques, developing simulation-based inference methods for parameter estimation of the most massive gravitational-wave sources. These systems lie at the edge of the parameter space covered by current waveform models, making them an ideal testbed for new inference approaches that combine population synthesis, machine learning, and gravitational-wave data analysis.
